The person behind the CV

Commerce strategist.
Human first.

I've spent the better part of a decade helping brands figure out how to sell things on the internet — and more importantly, why they should care. That sounds simple, but it rarely is. The interesting work is almost always about people and organisations, not platforms.

I'm based in London with my wife and daughter, who is four years old and has strong opinions about everything. She's also the reason I know rather more about coffee than any adult should — a double espresso at 6am has become a non-negotiable part of my morning ritual.

What I genuinely love about my work is the combination of complex problems and interesting people. Every client engagement starts with a puzzle: why isn't this channel performing, how do we move faster, what does this brand actually need to win? Getting to the real answer — not the obvious one — is what gets me out of bed. Then there's the human side: building trust with stakeholders, aligning teams, making the case for change in organisations that don't always find change easy.

Right now I'm thinking a lot about AI — specifically what it means for commerce as agents start to make purchasing decisions on behalf of consumers. The interface between brands and buyers is shifting in ways that most teams haven't fully reckoned with yet. That feels like an important problem to be close to.

If you're working on something interesting in this space — a replatform, a D2C strategy, an AI initiative — I'd love to hear about it.
